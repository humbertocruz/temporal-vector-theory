# Three-Dimensional Time as an Angular Geometry

## Conceptual statement

Temporal Vector Theory models fundamental time as a three-dimensional orientation space. Matter does not carry three clocks. It follows one effective temporal direction and experiences one proper-time parameter along that direction.

The complete temporal orientation is

```math
\mathbf n_T=(n_0,n_-,n_+),
\qquad
\|\mathbf n_T\|=1.
```

A direction in a three-dimensional space requires two angles:

```math
\mathbf n_T
=
(\cos\theta,
\sin\theta\cos\phi,
\sin\theta\sin\phi).
```

Therefore the minimal intuitive statement is:

```math
\boxed{\text{one clock, two temporal angles}}.
```

## Geometrical interpretation

The three temporal coordinates may be pictured metaphorically as temporal width, height, and depth. These labels do not mean that an observer can move freely between three independent past-future axes. They describe the ambient orientation space in which one causal direction is selected.

The proper-time parameter `τ` remains one-dimensional:

```math
X^A=X^A(\tau).
```

The temporal part of the tangent vector is oriented by `\mathbf n_T`.

## Meaning of the angles

### Inclination `θ`

```math
n_0=\cos\theta.
```

`θ` measures departure from the central temporal direction associated with our visible sector.

```math
N_T=\cos^2\theta
```

measures central overlap, while

```math
H_T=\sin^2\theta
```

measures hidden inclination.

Thus `θ` is provisionally the darkness angle.

### Hidden azimuth `φ`

`φ` selects direction inside the hidden temporal plane:

```math
n_-=\sin\theta\cos\phi,
\qquad
n_+=\sin\theta\sin\phi.
```

It determines whether a hidden orientation is negative, positive, or balanced.

The temporal charge is

```math
q_T=n_+^2-n_-^2
=-\sin^2\theta\cos(2\phi).
```

Thus `φ` is provisionally the polarity angle.

## Distinguished states

### Central visible state

```math
\theta=0,
\qquad
\mathbf n_T=(1,0,0).
```

The azimuth `φ` is undefined or irrelevant at the temporal pole.

### Pure negative hidden state

```math
\theta=\frac{\pi}{2},
\qquad
\phi=0,
\qquad
\mathbf n_T=(0,1,0).
```

### Pure positive hidden state

```math
\theta=\frac{\pi}{2},
\qquad
\phi=\frac{\pi}{2},
\qquad
\mathbf n_T=(0,0,1).
```

### Balanced hidden state

```math
\theta=\frac{\pi}{2},
\qquad
\phi=\frac{\pi}{4},
```

which gives equal hidden components and

```math
q_T=0,
\qquad
H_T=1.
```

This state is neutral in temporal charge but not central or visible.

## Effective spatial section

Temporal orientation is hypothesized to lock to a corresponding spatial triad:

```math
\mathbf E_i(\mathbf n_T)
=
n_0\mathbf e_{0i}
+n_-\mathbf e_{-i}
+n_+\mathbf e_{+i}.
```

The physical section experienced by matter is therefore

```math
(\tau,\mathbf E_1,\mathbf E_2,\mathbf E_3).
```

The complete geometry may contain more dimensions, but a stable system occupies one effective local `3+1` section.

## Interaction overlap

For mutually orthogonal sector triads, overlap with our visible section gives the amplitude

```math
\mathcal A_\gamma\propto\cos\theta.
```

The minimal electromagnetic relation is

```math
e_{\rm eff}=e\cos\theta.
```

Therefore

```math
\alpha_{\rm eff}=\alpha\cos^2\theta
```

and the hydrogen binding scale becomes

```math
E_H(\theta)=13.6\cos^4\theta\ {\rm eV}.
```

This relation currently serves as a geometric toy model. A complete theory must derive it from the photon and matter actions.

## Cosmological interpretation

The framework provisionally assumes that the complete temporal geometry exists in the primordial phase. At high temperature, orientations may be disordered or nearly symmetric. As the Universe cools, a temperature-dependent potential may select stable angular regions.

A schematic history is

```math
\text{hot symmetric temporal geometry}
\rightarrow
\text{orientation transition}
\rightarrow
\text{locked local }3+1\text{ sections}
\rightarrow
\text{freeze-out of abundances}.
```

The abundance freezes when

```math
\Gamma_{\rm orient}(T_*)\approx H(T_*).
```

The visible fraction is then calculated by integrating the frozen angular distribution over the atom-forming central cone.

## Terminology transition

Earlier documents use **temporal polarization**. In the revised language:

- temporal polarization becomes **temporal orientation**;
- hidden polarity becomes **temporal charge**;
- neutral sector becomes **central sector**;
- polarization angle becomes **temporal inclination**;
- positive and negative polarizations become **positive and negative temporal orientations**.

The earlier equations remain usable where they describe normalized components, but the new terminology avoids suggesting three clocks or treating central visibility as identical to zero charge.

## Scientific cautions

This angular formulation is a conceptual improvement, not experimental evidence. A viable theory must still establish:

1. a covariant metric and field content;
2. the origin and stability of one effective causal direction;
3. a gauge-invariant derivation of electromagnetic overlap;
4. a primordial potential and transition rates;
5. compatibility with precision tests of relativity and particle physics;
6. distinct falsifiable predictions.
