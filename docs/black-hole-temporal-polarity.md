# Black-Hole Temporal Polarity and Merger Signatures

## Status

This document extends the provisional temporal-polarity hypothesis to black holes and compact-object mergers. It does not claim that black holes are dark matter, that matter escapes from inside an event horizon, or that current gravitational-wave observations already show temporal polarity.

The purpose is to define testable quantities that could distinguish hidden temporal distortions if TVT develops a consistent strong-gravity limit.

## 1. One clock with temporal polarization

TVT does not assign three independent clocks to a physical system. It assigns one effective clock whose orientation may be polarized within a three-dimensional temporal space.

The pure reference states are

```math
\tau\mapsto 0,
\qquad
\sigma\mapsto -1,
\qquad
\rho\mapsto +1.
```

These values represent temporal-distortion polarity, not geometric coordinates, negative energy, reverse time, or causal direction.

A continuous family of intermediate polarizations exists between the pure states. Therefore the temporal state is not restricted to three discrete possibilities.

## 2. Spatial consequence of polarization

The polarized clock selects one effective spatial triad through temporal-spatial frame locking.

For an internal orientation

```math
\mathbf n=(n_\tau,n_\sigma,n_\rho),
```

with

```math
n_\tau^2+n_\sigma^2+n_\rho^2=1,
```

the accessible spatial basis is

```math
\mathbf E_i(\mathbf n)
=
n_\tau\mathbf e_{\tau i}
+n_\sigma\mathbf e_{\sigma i}
+n_\rho\mathbf e_{\rho i},
\qquad i=1,2,3.
```

Thus one polarized clock generates one local `3+1` section. Different polarizations generate different effective spatial triads.

## 3. Hidden magnitude and polarity

Define hidden magnitude

```math
H=n_\sigma^2+n_\rho^2=1-n_\tau^2.
```

For `H>0`, define temporal polarity

```math
P_T=
\frac{n_\rho^2-n_\sigma^2}
{n_\rho^2+n_\sigma^2}.
```

The pure cases are

```math
P_T=-1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{sigma-polarized clock},
```

```math
P_T=+1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{rho-polarized clock},
```

```math
P_T=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{balanced hidden polarization}.
```

The pair

```math
(H,P_T)
```

specifies how far the clock is tilted from the visible temporal reference and toward which hidden polarity.

## 4. Black-hole polarization variables

A black hole may be assigned effective distortion descriptors

```math
(H_{\mathrm{BH}},P_{\mathrm{BH}}).
```

These are additional to ordinary mass, angular momentum, and electric charge. Their existence must be demonstrated rather than assumed.

The limiting interpretations are

```math
P_{\mathrm{BH}}=-1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{sigma-polarized distortion},
```

```math
P_{\mathrm{BH}}=+1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{rho-polarized distortion},
```

```math
P_{\mathrm{BH}}=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{balanced hidden distortion}.
```

Intermediate values represent continuously mixed temporal polarizations.

## 5. Polarity is not causal escape

A change in temporal polarity does not by itself restore outward causal connectivity.

The horizon condition remains

```math
\mathcal C_{\mathrm{out}}=0.
```

Therefore

```math
P_{\mathrm{BH}}\rightarrow-P_{\mathrm{BH}}
```

or a local matter-sector reversal does not imply that matter can exit from inside the horizon.

Internal reversibility and causal reversibility are distinct.

## 6. Same-polarity and opposite-polarity mergers

Consider two merging black holes with descriptors

```math
(H_1,P_1),
\qquad
(H_2,P_2).
```

A minimal polarity-mismatch measure is

```math
\mathcal M_P
=
H_1H_2(1-P_1P_2).
```

For equal pure polarities,

```math
P_1P_2=1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\mathcal M_P=0.
```

For opposite pure polarities,

```math
P_1P_2=-1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\mathcal M_P=2H_1H_2.
```

This suggests the qualitative hypothesis

```math
\text{opposite-polarity merger}
\Longrightarrow
\text{stronger excitation of hidden orientation modes}.
```

Intermediate polarizations produce intermediate mismatch.

## 7. Energy accounting

A provisional merger balance is

```math
E_{\mathrm{initial}}
=
E_{\mathrm{remnant}}
+
E_{\mathrm{GW}}
+
E_{\mathrm{orient}}.
```

Here `E_orient` represents energy temporarily or permanently stored in hidden orientation or polarity modes.

A viable theory must keep this term small enough to satisfy existing inspiral-merger-ringdown consistency tests unless a statistically significant anomaly is observed.

## 8. Ringdown ansatz

The visible gravitational-wave strain may be modeled provisionally as

```math
h(t)
=
h_{\mathrm{GR}}(t)
+
A_-e^{-t/\tau_-}
\cos(\omega_-t+\phi_-)
+
A_+e^{-t/\tau_+}
\cos(\omega_+t+\phi_+).
```

The two additional families represent negative- and positive-polarity orientation modes.

Possible observables are

```math
A_-,\ A_+,\ \omega_-,\ \omega_+,\ \tau_-,\ \tau_+.
```

If the hidden reflection symmetry is exact, one may expect

```math
\omega_-=\omega_+,
\qquad
\tau_-=\tau_+,
```

while amplitudes encode the merger's polarity content.

If the symmetry is broken,

```math
\omega_-\neq\omega_+
```

or

```math
\tau_-\neq\tau_+,
```

could distinguish the two hidden directions observationally.

## 9. Polarity cancellation and release

For opposite-polarity progenitors, the remnant may have reduced net polarity:

```math
P_{\mathrm{final}}
\rightarrow0.
```

The difference between the initial polarized state and the final balanced state could be emitted through

- ordinary gravitational radiation;
- additional damped orientation modes;
- modified nonlinear gravitational memory;
- a hidden energy channel;
- altered remnant mass or spin consistency.

This does not require matter to escape from inside either horizon. The observable signal can arise from the exterior geometry while a common horizon forms and relaxes.

## 10. Same-polarity reinforcement

For progenitors with the same polarity, a provisional alternative is

```math
|P_{\mathrm{final}}|>0,
```

with a longer-lived polarized remnant or stronger excitation of one polarity family.

A statistical population might therefore separate into merger classes with different residual ringdown patterns.

## 11. Detection strategy

A future TVT test would proceed by

1. inferring masses and spins from the inspiral;
2. predicting the general-relativistic merger and ringdown;
3. fitting additional polarity modes without allowing arbitrary waveform freedom;
4. comparing same-form residuals across many events;
5. testing whether amplitudes cluster into negative, positive, balanced, or intermediate families;
6. checking energy balance and waveform consistency;
7. rejecting the model if the predicted pattern is absent at the required sensitivity.

## Provisional black-hole polarity principle

> A black hole may carry a polarized temporal distortion in addition to its ordinary macroscopic parameters. The distortion belongs to one effective clock and may vary continuously between negative, neutral, and positive hidden orientations. During a merger, mismatch between the progenitors' polarizations may excite additional transient modes without permitting causal escape from inside the horizon.
