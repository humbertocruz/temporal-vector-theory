# Testable Predictions and Falsification Roadmap

Temporal Vector Theory currently provides a reinterpretation of established relativistic relations. That is not enough. The research program must identify effects that cannot be absorbed into standard relativity or conventional field theory.

## Candidate observable classes

### 1. Precision-clock residuals

Search for reproducible deviations from standard relativistic time dilation that depend on energy, trajectory, orientation, particle type, or gravitational environment.

### 2. Particle–antiparticle asymmetries

Derive whether opposite hidden temporal orientations produce measurable differences beyond known CP-violating effects. Existing experimental limits must be treated as constraints, not invitations to improvise.

### 3. Propagation effects

Determine whether hidden temporal orientation changes dispersion, phase accumulation, polarization, oscillation, or arrival times for photons, neutrinos, or gravitational waves.

### 4. Dark-sector coupling

Construct a quantitative coupling law that can reproduce gravitational evidence attributed to dark matter while suppressing electromagnetic interaction.

### 5. Cosmological dynamics

Derive whether collective temporal geometry can mimic accelerated expansion without inserting an arbitrary effective dark-energy term.

### 6. Short-range gravity

Check whether hidden temporal degrees of freedom produce controlled departures from the inverse-square law at experimentally accessible scales.

## Minimum standard for a prediction

A proposed TVT effect must specify:

1. the observable quantity;
2. the standard-theory prediction;
3. the TVT correction term;
4. the parameter values and dimensions;
5. the experimental regime;
6. the result that would falsify the hypothesis.

## Immediate falsification conditions

TVT should be rejected or radically revised if its consistent formulation necessarily:

- introduces an experimentally excluded preferred frame;
- violates established Lorentz invariance without a viable suppression mechanism;
- creates propagating negative-energy ghosts;
- permits uncontrolled causal loops;
- contradicts precision tests already explained by relativity;
- adds no observable content beyond a change of coordinates.

## First research target

The most useful next result is a minimal covariant model that produces one small correction term while preserving the confirmed low-energy limit of special relativity.
