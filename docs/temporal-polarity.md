# Temporal Polarity

## Status

This document introduces a provisional physical distinction between the two hidden temporal directions of Temporal Vector Theory (TVT). The visible temporal axis is treated as a neutral reference, while the two hidden axes are interpreted as opposite polarities of temporal distortion.

This is a speculative ansatz. The labels negative and positive do not mean negative energy, backward time, or causal reversal.

## 1. Polarity assignment

The proposed internal polarity labels are

```math
\tau\mapsto 0,
\qquad
\sigma\mapsto -1,
\qquad
\rho\mapsto +1.
```

The value attached to each axis is not its geometric coordinate. It is an internal polarity carried by distortion toward that axis.

The visible sector is the neutral reference. The `sigma` and `rho` sectors are opposite orientations under a hidden reflection symmetry.

## 2. Hidden magnitude and polarity

Let an internal matter-sector orientation be

```math
\mathbf n=(n_\tau,n_\sigma,n_\rho),
\qquad
n_\tau^2+n_\sigma^2+n_\rho^2=1.
```

Define the hidden magnitude

```math
H=n_\sigma^2+n_\rho^2=1-n_\tau^2.
```

Its interpretation is

```math
H=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{pure visible-sector consistency},
```

```math
H\approx1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{predominantly hidden-sector consistency}.
```

For `H>0`, define temporal polarity

```math
P_T=
\frac{n_\rho^2-n_\sigma^2}
{n_\rho^2+n_\sigma^2}.
```

Then

```math
-1\leq P_T\leq1.
```

The limiting cases are

```math
P_T=-1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{pure sigma polarity},
```

```math
P_T=+1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{pure rho polarity},
```

```math
P_T=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{balanced hidden mixture}.
```

For the pure visible state `H=0`, hidden polarity is undefined rather than zero, because there is no hidden component to polarize.

## 3. Why two observables are necessary

Hidden magnitude and temporal polarity encode different information.

Two states can have the same hidden magnitude but opposite polarity:

```math
H_1=H_2,
\qquad
P_{T,1}=-P_{T,2}.
```

Likewise, a balanced fully hidden state can have

```math
H=1,
\qquad
P_T=0,
```

while visible matter has

```math
H=0.
```

Therefore polarity alone cannot measure darkness. The pair

```math
(H,P_T)
```

is the minimal provisional descriptor.

## 4. Reflection symmetry

The hidden-sector exchange

```math
\sigma\leftrightarrow\rho
```

acts as

```math
P_T\rightarrow-P_T,
\qquad
H\rightarrow H.
```

This defines a candidate discrete temporal-polarity symmetry.

A symmetric theory may assign equal masses and couplings to both polarities. A broken or spontaneously selected phase may allow states with different abundances or observable responses.

## 5. Relation to matter identity

Temporal polarity belongs provisionally to the internal matter-sector orientation `n`, not directly to ordinary kinematic temporal tilt `u_T`.

A rapidly moving visible particle may have a strongly tilted kinematic rail while retaining

```math
H_{\mathrm{matter}}\approx0.
```

A genuine dark-sector excitation instead has a stable internal state with

```math
H_{\mathrm{matter}}>0.
```

This distinction prevents ordinary relativistic motion from automatically converting visible matter into dark matter.

## 6. Minimal polarity interaction

For two systems with hidden magnitudes `H_1,H_2` and polarities `P_1,P_2`, a toy incompatibility energy is

```math
E_{\mathrm{pol}}
=
\lambda_P H_1H_2(1-P_1P_2),
```

with `lambda_P >= 0`.

For equal pure polarities,

```math
P_1P_2=1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
E_{\mathrm{pol}}=0.
```

For opposite pure polarities,

```math
P_1P_2=-1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
E_{\mathrm{pol}}=2\lambda_P H_1H_2.
```

This is not yet a derived interaction law. It is a minimal model showing how opposite hidden polarities could excite additional modes during violent interactions.

## 7. Cosmological interpretation

The two dark sectors need not be unrelated species. They may be opposite polarities of one hidden temporal distortion:

```math
\mathcal D_-
\leftrightarrow
\sigma,
\qquad
\mathcal D_+
\leftrightarrow
\rho.
```

Localized stable excitations of either polarity are dark-matter candidates. A balanced or spatially varying polarity background may contribute differently, potentially through inter-sector tension rather than localized matter.

No identification with observed dark matter or dark energy is claimed until a full stress-energy tensor and effective equation of state are derived.

## 8. Open requirements

The theory must still determine:

1. whether temporal polarity is fundamental, emergent, or gauge-redundant;
2. the action whose symmetry produces `P_T -> -P_T`;
3. whether the two polarities have equal masses and couplings;
4. whether domain walls or mixed-polarity defects exist;
5. how polarity contributes to the complete stress-energy tensor;
6. whether polarity is conserved or can be converted;
7. how polarity can be measured through gravitational phenomena.

## Provisional temporal-polarity principle

> The visible temporal direction is a neutral reference, while the two hidden temporal directions represent opposite polarities of temporal distortion. A physical hidden state is characterized minimally by its hidden magnitude and temporal polarity. Negative and positive polarity are internal orientation labels, not negative energy or reverse time.
