# Temporal Vector Theory

**Temporal Vector Theory (TVT)** is an exploratory hypothesis in which time is modeled as a three-dimensional orientation space. A physical system carries one effective clock, not three independent clocks. The clock may be polarized toward a neutral visible direction or toward two opposite hidden temporal polarities, and that polarization selects the system's effective spatial triad.

> This repository develops Temporal Vector Theory as an open, falsifiable, and mathematically explicit research program.

## Fundamental relation

```math
u_\tau^2 + u_\sigma^2 + u_\rho^2 = 1
```

with

```math
u_\tau = \sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}},
\qquad
u_\sigma = \frac{v}{c}\cos\varphi,
\qquad
u_\rho = \frac{v}{c}\sin\varphi.
```

## Core idea

- A physical system has one effective clock with a three-component temporal orientation.
- Observers perceive only a one-dimensional projection as proper time.
- Spatial velocity redistributes the clock orientation between observable time and hidden temporal directions.
- The temporal orientation and one effective spatial triad form a locked local `3+1` frame.
- The visible direction is provisionally neutral, while the two hidden directions are opposite temporal-distortion polarities.
- Continuous intermediate polarizations generate a continuous family of effective spatial triads.
- Matter identity, kinematic tilt, and gravitational transport must be represented by distinct variables where required.
- The theory must reproduce established relativity in its observable limit and produce additional falsifiable predictions.

## Trigonotime

**Trigonotime** is the provisional name for the study of projections, angles, rotations, polarizations, and distances between evolution vectors in a multidimensional temporal space.

```math
u_\tau=\cos\theta,
\qquad
u_\sigma=\sin\theta\cos\varphi,
\qquad
u_\rho=\sin\theta\sin\varphi.
```

## Temporal polarity

The provisional polarity labels are

```math
\tau\mapsto0,
\qquad
\sigma\mapsto-1,
\qquad
\rho\mapsto+1.
```

They are internal labels for opposite hidden distortions. They do not represent negative energy, reverse time, or geometric coordinates.

A hidden state is described minimally by hidden magnitude and polarity:

```math
H=n_\sigma^2+n_\rho^2,
```

```math
P_T=
\frac{n_\rho^2-n_\sigma^2}
{n_\rho^2+n_\sigma^2},
\qquad H>0.
```
